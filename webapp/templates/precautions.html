Monkeypox Prevention

Protect yourself and others with these important safety measures

Hand Hygiene

Wash hands frequently with soap and water for at least 20 seconds or use an alcohol-based hand sanitizer.

Social Distancing

Maintain physical distance from people who are sick or have symptoms of monkeypox.

Safe Practices

Avoid contact with animals that could harbor the virus and materials they have touched.

Protective Equipment

Wear appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) when caring for infected individuals.

Vaccination

Consider vaccination if you're at high risk of exposure to monkeypox.

Early Detection

Be aware of the symptoms and seek medical attention if you suspect exposure.

Recognizing Symptoms

Early Symptoms (0-5 days)
  • Fever
  • Headache
  • Muscle aches
  • Backache
  • Swollen lymph nodes
  • Chills
  • Exhaustion
Rash (1-3 days after fever)
  • Often begins on the face then spreads
  • Lesions progress through stages
  • May be painful or itchy
  • Scabs form and fall off

If You're Infected

Isolate Yourself

Stay in a separate room and use a separate bathroom if possible. Avoid contact with others and pets.

Care for Your Rash

Keep the rash clean and covered with bandages. Avoid scratching or touching the rash.

Contact Healthcare Provider

Call ahead before visiting a healthcare facility and inform them about your symptoms and possible exposure.

Prevent Spread

Wear a mask, cover your mouth when coughing or sneezing, and practice good hand hygiene.

Seek Emergency Medical Attention If You Experience:
  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Chest pain or pressure
  • New confusion or inability to arouse
  • Bluish lips or face
  • Severe pain or the rash is near your eyes, mouth, or genitals

Remember

While monkeypox can be serious, most people recover within 2-4 weeks. Early detection and proper care can help prevent complications and reduce the risk of spreading the virus to others.

Stay informed, stay safe, and don't hesitate to seek medical advice if you have concerns about your health.
