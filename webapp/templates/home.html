About Monkeypox

Monkeypox is a rare disease caused by infection with the monkeypox virus. It can be transmitted from animals to humans and between humans.

Common Symptoms:
  • Fever
  • Headache
  • Muscle aches
  • Swollen lymph nodes
  • Chills
  • Exhaustion
  • Rash that can look like pimples or blisters
Did you know? Monkeypox is usually a self-limited disease with symptoms lasting 2-4 weeks.

Prevention Tips

Practice Good Hygiene

Wash your hands frequently with soap and water or use an alcohol-based hand sanitizer.

Avoid Close Contact

Avoid close contact with people who have a rash that looks like monkeypox.

Handle Animals Safely

Avoid contact with animals that could harbor the virus, especially animals that are sick or found dead.

Use Personal Protective Equipment

Wear appropriate personal protective equipment when caring for infected individuals.
